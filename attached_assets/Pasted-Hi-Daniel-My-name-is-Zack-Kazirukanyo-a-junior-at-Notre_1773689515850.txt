Stack & Hosting
FoundFolio is a React + Vite web application hosted on Vercel. The backend runs on Supabase, which provides PostgreSQL, authentication, object storage for item photos, and edge functions.
Data and authentication are hosted on Supabase’s managed infrastructure (AWS us-east-1). Supabase maintains SOC 2 Type II certification.
Access Control

Rather than running a separate API server, we’ve pushed most authorization down to the database using PostgreSQL Row-Level Security. Policies are scoped by campus, building, and role.
Authentication runs through Google OAuth via Supabase Auth. The goal there was simply to avoid managing credentials ourselves.
Administrative access is controlled through an allowlisted staff invitation table. Removing a user from that table effectively removes elevated access even if they authenticate again through OAuth.

Multi-Campus Design
The schema is partitioned by campus and building. That keeps authorization boundaries explicit in the data model and lets us support additional campuses without major structural changes.

Data Stored
At the moment we store only operational lost-and-found data: item descriptions, timestamps, building/location information, and item photos. When a student submits a claim, we collect contact information (email or phone) to coordinate pickup.
We’ve intentionally kept the data surface fairly narrow and do not store financial data, academic records, or other sensitive institutional data.
Areas We’re Still Working On

There are quite a few areas we're continuing to improve as the platform matures. On the operational side, we're working on expanding audit logging for administrative actions, formalizing the staff offboarding and access revocation process, and improving monitoring around authentication and role transitions. On the infrastructure side, we're building in periodic RLS policy reviews as the schema evolves and addressing image lifecycle management so that photos tied to closed or deleted items are cleaned up over time. We recognize these are gaps worth closing and are treating them as active priorities rather than future considerations.

We've also given some thought to FERPA applicability. Our current understanding is that operational lost-and-found records likely don't constitute education records under FERPA, but we'd welcome your guidance on how they should be classified.
